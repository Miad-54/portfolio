About Me

Where Engineering Meets Reality

I grew up in Barishal. I chose Industrial Engineering because it's fundamentally about one question: why isn't this working as well as it could? That question is harder to answer than it sounds — and it never stays inside factories.

I've followed it into beverage plants trying to find where throughput was bleeding. Into supply chain models mapping why 40% of Bangladesh's mango harvest never reaches a consumer. Into boardrooms where a company had bought a perfectly good digital solution and wasn't using it — because the gap between software and operations is a human problem, not a technical one.

That gap is what I work in. Right now, I consult on B2B digital solutions for manufacturing — which means I sit with engineers and operators, figure out what's actually broken, translate that into something buildable, and stay until it works. Most people hand off at the proposal stage. I don't.

I'm not trying to be the person who knows everything about any single thing. I'm trying to be the person who can look at a system, see what's wrong, and move it.
